Thesis Project

Monitoring and Classification of Nitrate Sufficiency of lettuce an Aquaponics System based on Morphological Status using a Machine Vision Approach

This study has successfully developed a machine vision-based monitoring and classification system for assessing nitrate sufficiency in aquaponics based on the morphological status of lettuce. The project achieved its specific aims as follows: Firstly, an extensive image dataset of lettuce plants under varying nitrate conditions in an aquaponic system was collected, including both nitrate-deficient and nitrate-sufficient conditions. Secondly, the study identified the top-performing algorithm suitable for classifying and segmenting the lettuce's morphological status with a high degree of accuracy. Finally, the system's capabilities were put into practical use by controlling the frequency of the fish feeding mechanism based on the model's inference results. This regulation effectively managed nitrate levels in the water, ensuring optimal conditions for lettuce growth. In conclusion, this project has successfully created a sophisticated machine vision-based solution that enhances the management of nitrate levels in aquaponics, ultimately promoting the health and productivity of lettuce crops.
